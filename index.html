Roald Dahl

Roald Dahl (1916-1990) was a prolific British author renowned for his imaginative and whimsical storytelling. Born in Wales to Norwegian parents, Dahl's works span various genres, including children's literature, short stories, and adult fiction. Some of his most beloved and enduring works include "Charlie and the Chocolate Factory," "Matilda," and "James and the Giant Peach." Dahl's writing is characterized by its dark humor, clever plot twists, and memorable characters. His ability to capture the imaginations of both children and adults has solidified his legacy as one of the greatest storytellers of the 20th century. Dahl's works continue to enchant readers worldwide, and his impact on literature and popular culture endures.
